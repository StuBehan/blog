Hi, I'm Stu Behan - Technical Lead at [StackOne](https://www.stackone.com/), working on backend architecture, applied AI, and the plumbing that lets AI agents actually *do* things in the real world.

StackOne is integration infrastructure for AI agents: a single MCP server that gives an agent OAuth-managed access to hundreds of enterprise systems, with responses shaped to stay token-efficient - full payloads to your app, lean summaries to the agent. I part of the team building that. Day to day I own architecture decisions across Falcon, our execution engine that handles auth, retries, errors and data transformation, and I care a lot about performance, reliability, and what it's actually like to build against us - alongside mentoring the engineers around me through design sessions and reviews.

I joined StackOne as a backend engineer back in 2023, building the systems that talk to hundreds of third-party APIs without falling over: concurrency and rate-limiting with Redis and BullMQ, caching that degrades gracefully, event-driven workflows, and a lot of developer tooling and tests to keep it all clean and tidy. Before that I was a Technical Solutions Engineer at Yieldify, building TypeScript solutions for eCommerce clients and the internal tooling to support them.

These days a fair bit of what I make is for the way I actually work - tooling for AI coding agents. [Stack Nudge](https://github.com/StackOneHQ/stack-nudge) pings me when an agent needs my attention, [StackVox](https://github.com/StackOneHQ/stackvox) is an offline text-to-speech engine (it's what reads these posts aloud), and I look after a few internal Claude Code plugins.

This is where I write things down - mostly notes on AI-assisted development and developer workflow, with the occasional detour into whatever I've been measuring (like whether my plug-in hybrid is actually cheaper to run).

You can find me on [GitHub](https://github.com/StuBehan) and [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/stu-baines-dinning).
